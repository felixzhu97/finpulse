# Business Model & Monetization

This document describes revenue streams and commercialization strategies for the portfolio analytics platform.

## Revenue Streams

### 1. Subscription / SaaS

| Tier | Target | Features |
|------|--------|----------|
| Free | Casual users | Basic portfolio view, limited history |
| Pro | Active investors | Full analytics, AI risk/VaR, sentiment, history |
| Premium | Power users | Priority support, advanced AI, custom reports |

### 2. Transaction Commission

- Revenue share from broker integration when users trade via the platform.
- Per-trade fee or percentage of order value.

### 3. Asset Management Fee

- AUM-based fee for managed portfolios or robo-advisory.
- Example: 0.25%–0.50% per annum on assets under management.

### 4. Value-Added Services

- AI analytics: VaR, fraud detection, sentiment, forecast.
- Advanced risk metrics and portfolio optimization.
- Premium market data and valuations.

### 5. Data & API

- Sell market data, risk metrics, or analytics to third parties.
- Developer API access with usage-based pricing.

### 6. White-Label / B2B

- License the platform to banks, brokers, or wealth managers.
- Annual or per-user licensing fee.

### 7. Advertising & Referrals

- Promotional placement for brokers, funds, or financial products.
- Affiliate revenue from referred sign-ups (where compliant).

## Target Segments

| Segment | Needs | Monetization Fit |
|---------|-------|------------------|
| Retail investors | Portfolio tracking, insights | Subscription, ads |
| Active traders | Real-time, risk tools | Subscription, commission |
| Institutions | API, white-label | B2B, data/API |
| Advisors | Client portfolios, reports | White-label, premium features |

## Pricing Considerations

- Free tier to drive adoption.
- Clear value ladder from Free → Pro → Premium.
- Volume or usage discounts for B2B and API.
- Localized pricing and compliance per region.
